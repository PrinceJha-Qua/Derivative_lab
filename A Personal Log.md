This project is my attempt to understand derivatives from first principles by building them computationally.

Instead of treating options as abstract formulas, this repository explores how derivative contracts behave through visualization, numerical models, and experimentation.

The goal is not just to “implement pricing models,” but to build intuition for concepts such as:

- payoff vs price
- no-arbitrage
- replication and hedging
- convexity
- volatility sensitivity
- time value decay
- uncertainty pricing

This repository is being built as a learning laboratory rather than a polished production system.

Many sections intentionally document:

- misconceptions,
- failed assumptions,
- intermediate experiments,
- and intuition gained during the process.

The project currently focuses on:

- option payoff visualization
- binomial option pricing
- replication-based pricing intuition
- put-call parity exploration

Over time, the architecture is designed to grow into a larger derivatives research environment including:

- Greeks visualization
- implied volatility
- Monte Carlo pricing
- delta hedging simulations
- volatility surfaces
- strategy analysis

The emphasis throughout the project is clarity over complexity.

Every model implemented here is meant to be understood deeply enough to explain, modify, and extend, not merely copied.
